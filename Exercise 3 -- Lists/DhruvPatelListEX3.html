My Equity Lists

What To Priortize To Ensure Educational Equity

  1. One-on-one Support. Countless studies have shown the power that individualized mentorship and support holds to a student's academic success -- particularly in that students bond to their teachers on a personal level. It's important to allow for this in our education system, as many students don't always have bolstered support systems, and by ensuring one-on-one support, we can augment this dimension.

  2. Diverse Curriculum. It's important to have course and program offerings that are catered to a wide audience, rather than having one-dimensional servies. For example, a school may implement an African-American History class, where students learn about the tragidies and triumphs in African-American history. Having such courses would certainly allow for inclusion, diversity, equity.

How To Achieve Educational Equity

  • Hold All Accountable
    • If a student, adminstrator, or any other indivudal engages with inherently racist actions, he or she should be held accountable and proper action must be taken


  • Inclusion And Access
    • Ensure that all school-sponsored programs are accessible to all indivudals, regardless of their socioeconomic status. Mandate all school clubs and programs to be inclusive of all students, rather than a specific set.


  • Expectations For All
    • Expectations for a student SHOULD NOT be set on the basis of their race, ethnicity, financial status, or any other quality of the sort -- ensure that this ideology is reflected in the curriculum and how teachers teach.

IMSA's Educational Equity Policy + Key Highlights

  • With the events and protests that have occured over the last summer, IMSA has taken major actions to ensure educational equity -- here are some highlights of IMSA's approach:

    • Conducting Research to best understand how to support CLED students -- with research, develop equitable policies meeting the needs of all, regardless of socioeconomic and sociopolitical factors.

    • Diversity Training -- both juniors and seniors engage with diversity trainings where they watch a specific movie and discuss prominent messages from the video

    • Brotherhood/Sister Circle - this "proactive, intentional and ongoing" cultivates a bias-free, embracive enviorment where indivudals are able to discuss sociopoltical issues, both of the present and of the past

Key Definitions

Here are four key definitions regarding social justice that are important to know!

Equity
"A process and a outcome" that holds all who experience power and systemic privilege accountable for their actions and ensures the fair and just distribution/access of resources.
Microaggresions
Intentional or unintentional remark targeted towards those of historically marginalized groups.
Racial Bias
The notion of one race being inferior to another.
Diversity
Recognizing and embracing all for who they are, rather than forcing one to fit a specific mold (Note: diversity includes nuerodiversity).
